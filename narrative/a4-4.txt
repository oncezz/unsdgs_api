In conclusion, trade barriers typically introduce inefficiencies and distort household income patterns. Conventional trade agreements that reverse these may indirectly promote economic and social SDGs.<br><br>
Newer generations of trade agreements include provisions that also promote SDGs more directly.<br><br>
Some of these provisions satisfy the “low-hanging fruit” principle introduced in Module A.2, lowering the cost to achieve some SDG targets. However, some controversies remain, such as IPR issues or the use of labor/environmental provisions as disguised trade barriers.<br><br>
