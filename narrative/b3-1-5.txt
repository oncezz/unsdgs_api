In conclusion, there is no simple answer to the question of whether trade lowers labour standards. Openness to trade increases the demand for workers, which creates jobs and raises workers' income but, at the same time, incentivizes exploitation when the penalties for doing so are low. Labour exploitation is a symptom of weak institutions in a country, and trade liberalization can exacerbate that problem. Modern-day trade agreements then try to address this concern by adding provisions that promote stronger domestic institutions alongside trade liberalization.<br><br>
