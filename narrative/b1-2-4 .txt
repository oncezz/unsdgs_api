Summing up, food trade is a usually reliable instrument to smooth supply shocks and price shocks; this promotes availability and stabilizes food markets.<br><br>

In net food-importers, limiting trade to achieve self-sufficiency can undermine food security.<br><br> 

To achieve important Sustainable Development Goals, including ending hunger and reducing poverty, trade can be an ally. This is especially true when accompanied by domestic policies to improve agricultural productivity - and enhance opportunities for rural labour to move to areas of higher productivity, such as manufacturing.<br><br>
