Brief quiz answers

  1. (a), (c), (d). Hourly pay reflects productivity but is unlikely to cause it.

  2. False, or at least unlikely. Less competitive firms will exit the market, but more competitive ones will improve their efficiency. Exit by under-performers will also help labor and other resources move to more productive opportunities.

  3. (c). Sectoral TFP growth has been minimal; most gains have come by moving resources from less to more productive activities.

  4. True. Greater openness encouraged investment, especially in manufacturing. This induced movement of labor to the manufacturing sector, where labor productivity is higher. The net effect was higher overall labor productivity
